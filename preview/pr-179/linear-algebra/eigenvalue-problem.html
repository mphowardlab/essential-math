3.6. Eigenvalue problem#

3.6.1. Skill Builder Problems#

Find the eigenvalues and eigenvectors of the following matrices:

  1. (3.81)#\[\begin{equation} \vv{A} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 2 \\ 0 & -4 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation}\]
Solution

Since A is upper triangular, the eigenvalues are the diagonal entries:

(3.82)#\[\begin{align} \lambda_1 &= 4 \\ \lambda_2 &= -4 \end{align}\]

Then, use these eigenvalues to find the eigenvectors:

(3.83)#\[\begin{equation} \lambda_1 = 4: \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 2 \\ 0 & -8 \end{bmatrix} \vv{x_1} = 0 \longrightarrow \vv{x_1} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation}\]
(3.84)#\[\begin{equation} \lambda_2 = -4: \begin{bmatrix} 8 & 2 \\ 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \vv{x_2} = 0 \longrightarrow \vv{x_2} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation}\]

So.

(3.85)#\[\begin{align} \lambda_1 &= 4 \\ \lambda_2 &= -4 \\ \vv{x_1} &= \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \\ \vv{x_2} &= \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix} \end{align}\]
  1. (3.86)#\[\begin{equation} \vv{A} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & -2 \\ 9 & -6 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation}\]
Solution

First, find the eigenvalues:

(3.87)#\[\begin{equation} |\vv{A}-\lambda \vv{I}| = \begin{vmatrix} 5 - \lambda & -2 \\ 9 & -6 - \lambda \end{vmatrix} \end{equation}\]
(3.88)#\[\begin{equation} (\lambda - 5)(\lambda + 6) + 18 = \lambda^2 + \lambda - 12 = (\lambda + 4)(\lambda - 3) = 0 \end{equation}\]
(3.89)#\[\begin{align} \lambda_1 &= 3 \\ \lambda_2 &= -4 \end{align}\]

Then, use these eigenvalues to find the eigenvectors:

(3.90)#\[\begin{equation} \lambda_1 = 3: \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -2 \\ 9 & -9 \end{bmatrix} \vv{x_1} = 0 \longrightarrow \vv{x_1} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation}\]
(3.91)#\[\begin{equation} \lambda_2 = -4: \begin{bmatrix} 9 & -2 \\ 9 & -2 \end{bmatrix} \vv{x_2} = 0 \longrightarrow \vv{x_2} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 9 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation}\]

So,

(3.92)#\[\begin{align} \lambda_1 &= 3 \\ \lambda_2 &= -4 \\ \vv{x_1} &= \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \\ \vv{x_2} &= \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 9 \end{bmatrix} \\ \end{align}\]
  1. (3.93)#\[\begin{equation} \vv{A} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 2 & -2 \\ 2 & 5 & 0 \\ -2 & 0 & 7 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation}\]
Solution

First, find the eigenvalues:

(3.94)#\[\begin{align} |\vv{A}-\lambda \vv{I}| &= \begin{vmatrix} 6 - \lambda & 2 & -2 \\ 2 & 5 - \lambda & 0 \\ -2 & 0 & 7 - \lambda \end{vmatrix} \\ &= -2 \begin{vmatrix} 2 & -2 \\ 0 & 7 - \lambda \end{vmatrix} + (5 - \lambda) \begin{vmatrix} 6 - \lambda & -2 \\ -2 & 7 - \lambda \end{vmatrix} \\ &= (-2)(2)(7 - \lambda) + (5 - \lambda)[(\lambda - 6)(\lambda - 7) - 4] \\ &= -28 + 4\lambda + (5 - \lambda)\left(\lambda^2 - 10\lambda + 38 \right) \\ &= -\lambda^3 + 18\lambda^2 - 99\lambda + 162 = 0 \end{align}\]
(3.95)#\[\begin{align} \lambda_1 &= 9 \\ \lambda_2 &= 6 \\ \lambda_3 &= 3 \end{align}\]

Then, use these eigenvalues to find the eigenvectors:

(3.96)#\[\begin{equation} \lambda_1 = 9: \begin{bmatrix} -3 & 2 & -2 \\ 2 & -4 & 0 \\ -2 & 0 & -2 \end{bmatrix} \vv{x_1} = 0 \end{equation}\]

Reduce the row and choose:

(3.97)#\[\begin{align} x_3 &= 2 \end{align}\]
(3.98)#\[\begin{equation} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -2 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \vv{x_1} = 0 \longrightarrow \vv{x_1} = \begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ -1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation}\]
(3.99)#\[\begin{equation} \lambda_2 = 6: \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 2 & -2 \\ 2 & -1 & 0 \\ -2 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \vv{x_2} = 0 \end{equation}\]

Reduce the row and choose:

(3.100)#\[\begin{align} x_3 &= 2 \end{align}\]
(3.101)#\[\begin{equation} \begin{bmatrix} -2 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \vv{x_2} = 0 \longrightarrow \vv{x_2} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation}\]
(3.102)#\[\begin{equation} \lambda_3 = 3: \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 2 & -2 \\ 2 & 2 & 0 \\ -2 & 0 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \vv{x_3} = 0 \end{equation}\]

Reduce the row and choose:

(3.103)#\[\begin{align} x_3 &= 1 \end{align}\]
(3.104)#\[\begin{equation} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \vv{x_3} = 0 \longrightarrow \vv{x_3} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation}\]

So,

(3.105)#\[\begin{align} \lambda_1 &= 9 \\ \lambda_2 &= 6 \\ \lambda_3 &= 3 \\ \vv{x_1} &= \begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ -1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \\ \vv{x_2} &= \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \\ \vv{x_3} &= \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \end{align}\]
