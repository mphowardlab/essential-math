1.9. Partial fraction decomposition#

1.9.1. Shortcut: Heaviside Cover-up Method#

The Heaviside cover-up method is a simplified approach to finding the coefficients in partial fraction decomposition for linear factors. Its main advantage is avoiding cross-multiplication, which can be time-consuming, by covering up a factor and substituting the appropriate value into the expression.

1.9.1.1. Example:#

Given:

(1.35)#\[\begin{equation} \frac{3x+11}{(x-3)(x+2)} = \frac{A_1}{x-3} + \frac{A_2}{x+2} \end{equation}\]

Multiply by a factor and take the limit to find \(A_1\) and \(A_2\):

(1.36)#\[\begin{align} \lim_{x\to 3} \left( \frac{3x+11}{x+2} = A_1 + \frac{A_2(x-3)}{x+2} \right) \\ \frac{3(3)+11}{3+2} = A_1 \\ \end{align}\]
\[ A_1 = 4 \]
(1.37)#\[\begin{align} \lim_{x\to -2} \left( \frac{3x+11}{x-3} = \frac{A_1(x+2)}{x-3} + A_2 \right) \\ \frac{3(-2)+11}{-2-3} = A_2 \\ \end{align} \]
\[ A_2 = -1 \]

The Heaviside cover-up method only works for the highest powers of linear factors.

1.9.1.2. Example: Incomplete Decomposition#

Given:

(1.38)#\[\begin{equation} \frac{x^2-29x+5}{(x-4)^2 (x^2 +3)} = \frac{A_1}{x-4} + \frac{A_2}{(x-4)^2} + \frac{A_3x + B_3}{x^2 +3} \end{equation}\]

\(A_2\) can be solved using the Heaviside cover-up method because it corresponds to \((x-4)^2\).

(1.39)#\[\begin{equation} \frac{(4)^2 - 29(4) + 5}{(4)^2 + 3} = A_2 \\ \end{equation}\]
\[ A_2 = -5 \]

1.9.2. Polynomial Division#

Polynomial division is a method used to simplify rational functions when the degree of the numerator (P) is higher than the degree of the denominator (Q).

1.9.2.1. Example:#

Given:

(1.40)#\[\begin{equation} y' = \frac{x^4 - 5x^3 + 6x^2 -18}{x^3 -3x^2} \end{equation}\]

P is a higher degree than Q, so polynomial division can be performed.

\[\begin{split} \begin{array}{r} x-2\\ x^3-3x^2{\overline{\smash{\big)}\, x^4-5x^3+6x^2-18}} \\ \underline{-~(x^4-3x^3)}\phantom{+6x^2-18} \\ -2x^3+6x^2-18 \\ \underline{-~(-2x^3+6x^2)}\phantom{-18} \\ -18 \\ \end{array} \end{split}\]
(1.41)#\[\begin{equation} y'= x-2- \frac{18}{x^3-3x^2} \end{equation}\]

Use partial fraction decomposition for the term \(\frac{-18}{x^3-3x^2}\):

(1.42)#\[\begin{align} \frac{-18}{x^3-3x^2} = \frac{-18}{(x^2)(x-3)} = \frac{A_1}{x} + \frac{A_2}{x^2} + \frac{A_3}{x-3} \end{align}\]

Find \(A_2\) by heaviside cover-up method:

(1.43)#\[\begin{align} \frac{-18}{0-3}= A_2 \\ \end{align}\]
\[ A_2 = 6 \]

Find \(A_3\) by heaviside cover-up method:

(1.44)#\[\begin{align} \frac{-18}{3^2} = A_3 \\ \end{align}\]
\[ A_3 = -2 \]

Find \(A_1\) by substituting \(A_2\) and \(A_3\):

(1.45)#\[\begin{equation} -18 = A_1x(x-3) + 6(x-3) - 2x^2 \end{equation}\]

Plug in \(x=1\):

(1.46)#\[\begin{align} -18 = A_1(-2) + 6(-2) - 2 = -2A_1 - 14 \\ -4 = -2A_1 \\ \end{align}\]
\[ A_1 = 2 \]

Integrate:

(1.47)#\[\begin{align} y = \int (x - 2 + \frac{2}{x} + \frac{6}{x^2} - \frac{2}{x-3}) \, dx \\ = \frac{x^2}{2} -2x + 2\ln|x| - \frac{6}{x} - 2\ln|x-3| + C \end{align}\]
