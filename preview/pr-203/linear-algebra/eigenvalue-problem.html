3.6. Eigenvalue problem#

3.6.1. Skill builder problems#

Find the eigenvalues and eigenvectors

  1. For:

    (3.81)#\[\begin{equation} \vv{A} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 2 \\ 0 & -4 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation}\]
    Solution

    Since A is upper triangular, the eigenvalues are the diagonal entries, \(\lambda_1 = 4\) and \(\lambda_2 = -4\). Then, use these eigenvalues to find the eigenvectors that solve \((\vv{A}-\lambda \vv{I})\vv{x} = \vv{0}\).

    For \(\lambda_1 = 4\),

    (3.82)#\[\begin{equation} \vv{A}-\lambda_1\vv{I} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 2 \\ 0 & -8 \end{bmatrix} \to \vv{x}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation}\]

    For \(\lambda_2 = -4\),

    (3.83)#\[\begin{equation} \vv{A}-\lambda_2\vv{I} = \begin{bmatrix} 8 & 2 \\ 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \to \vv{x}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation}\]

  2. For:

    (3.84)#\[\begin{equation} \vv{A} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & -2 \\ 9 & -6 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation}\]
    Solution

    First, find the eigenvalues:

    (3.85)#\[\begin{align} |\vv{A}-\lambda \vv{I}| &= \begin{vmatrix} 5 - \lambda & -2 \\ 9 & -6 - \lambda \end{vmatrix} \\ &= (\lambda - 5)(\lambda + 6) + 18 \\ &= \lambda^2 + \lambda - 12 \\ &= (\lambda - 3)(\lambda + 4) = 0 \end{align}\]

    So, \(\lambda_1 = 3\) and \(\lambda_2 = -4\). For \(\lambda_1 = 4\),

    (3.86)#\[\begin{equation} \vv{A}-\lambda_1\vv{I} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -2 \\ 9 & -9 \end{bmatrix} \to \vv{x}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation}\]

    For \(\lambda_2 = -4\),

    (3.87)#\[\begin{equation} \vv{A}-\lambda_2\vv{I} = \begin{bmatrix} 9 & -2 \\ 9 & -2 \end{bmatrix} \to \vv{x}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 9 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation}\]

  3. For:

    (3.88)#\[\begin{equation} \vv{A} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 2 & -2 \\ 2 & 5 & 0 \\ -2 & 0 & 7 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation}\]
    Solution

    First, find the eigenvalues:

    (3.89)#\[\begin{align} |\vv{A}-\lambda \vv{I}| &= \begin{vmatrix} 6 - \lambda & 2 & -2 \\ 2 & 5 - \lambda & 0 \\ -2 & 0 & 7 - \lambda \end{vmatrix} \\ &= -2 \begin{vmatrix} 2 & -2 \\ 0 & 7 - \lambda \end{vmatrix} + (5 - \lambda) \begin{vmatrix} 6 - \lambda & -2 \\ -2 & 7 - \lambda \end{vmatrix} \\ &= -2 \cdot 2(7 - \lambda) + (5 - \lambda)[(\lambda - 6)(\lambda - 7) - 4] \\ &= -\lambda^3 + 18\lambda^2 - 99\lambda + 162 = 0 \end{align}\]

    Solving for these roots numerically gives \(\lambda_1 = 9\), \(\lambda_2 = 6\), and \(\lambda_3 = 3\).

    For \(\lambda_1 = 9\), using row reduction gives

    (3.90)#\[\begin{equation} \vv{A}-\lambda_1\vv{I} = \begin{bmatrix} -3 & 2 & -2 \\ 2 & -4 & 0 \\ -2 & 0 & -2 \end{bmatrix} \to\to \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation}\]

    Choosing \(x_3 = 2\) as the free variable gives

    (3.91)#\[\begin{equation} \vv{x}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ -1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation}\]

    For \(\lambda_2 = 6\), using row reduction gives

    (3.92)#\[\begin{equation} \vv{A}-\lambda_2\vv{I} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 2 & -2 \\ 2 & -1 & 0 \\ -2 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \to\to \begin{bmatrix} -2 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation}\]

    Taking \(x_3 = 2\) as the free v ariable gives:

    (3.93)#\[\begin{equation} \vv{x}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation}\]

    For \(\lambda_3 = 3\), using row reduction gives

    (3.94)#\[\begin{equation} \vv{A}-\lambda_3\vv{I} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 2 & -2 \\ 2 & 2 & 0 \\ -2 & 0 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \to\to \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -2 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation}\]

    Choosing \(x_3 = 1\) as the free variable gives:

    (3.95)#\[\begin{equation} \vv{x}_3 = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation}\]
