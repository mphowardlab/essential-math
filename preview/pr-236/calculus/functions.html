1.1. Functions#

1.1.1. 1. What is a function?#

A rule “f” that assigns a \textbf{unique} value \(f(x)\) to an input \(x\)

(1.1)#\[\begin{align} \text{x: independent variable} \hspace{2cm} \text{domain: all possible x}\\ \text{y: dependent variable} \hspace{2.2cm} \text{range: all possible y} \end{align}\]

A function can be represented by:

  • An equation: \begin{equation} f(x) = x^2 \end{equation}

  • A table: \begin{array}{c|c} x & f(x) \ \hline 0 & 0 \ 1 & 1 \ 2 & 4 \end{array}

  • A graph: [parabola graph representation]

  • A description: “square x”

\textbf{Not all curves are functions!} f must uniquely map x

(1.2)#\[\begin{align} x^2 + y^2 = 4 \text{ [circle equation]} \end{align}\]

\textbf{Vertical line test:} a vertical line cannot intersect a function twice

1.1.2. 2. Common functions#

1.1.2.1. Linear functions#

(1.3)#\[\begin{align} y = mx + b \text{ ("lines")} \end{align}\]

  • m: slope

  • b: intercept

1.1.2.2. Power functions#

(1.4)#\[\begin{align} y = kx^a \text{ ("power law")} \end{align}\]

  • k: prefactor

  • a: power

1.1.2.3. Polynomials#

(1.5)#\[\begin{align} y = a_n x^n + a_{n-1}x^{n-1} + \ldots + a_1x + a_0 \end{align}\]

  • n: degree

(1.6)#\[\begin{align} n=1&: \text{linear}\\ n=2&: \text{quadratic}\\ n=3&: \text{cubic} \end{align}\]

1.1.2.4. Exponential#

(1.7)#\[\begin{align} y = a^x \text{ (a > 0, a \neq 1)} \end{align}\]

  • a: base

Special case: a = e (≈ 2.718…) = Euler’s number

1.1.2.5. Logarithmic#

(1.8)#\[\begin{align} y = \log_a x \text{ (a > 0, a \neq 1)} \end{align}\]

Special cases:

(1.9)#\[\begin{align} a = e &\Rightarrow \log_e = \ln = \text{natural logarithm}\\ a = 10 &\Rightarrow \log_{10} = \log = \text{common logarithm} \end{align}\]

1.1.2.6. Trigonometric#

(1.10)#\[\begin{align} \sin x \hspace{1cm} \csc x\\ \cos x \hspace{1cm} \sec x\\ \tan x \hspace{1cm} \cot x \end{align}\]

1.1.3. Manipulating functions#

Horizontal shift

  • \(f(x+a)\) shifts f left by a.

  • \(f(x-a)\) shifts f right by a.

Vertical shift

  • \(f(x)+b\) shifts f up by b.

  • \(f(x)-b\) shifts f down by b.

Horizontal scaling

  • Scaling \(f(cx)\) compresses f horizontally by c.

  • Scaling \(f(x/c)\) stretches f horizontally by c.

Vertical scaling

  • Scaling \(f(x)/c\) compresses f vertically by c.

  • Scaling \(cf(x)\) streches f vertically by c.

1.1.4. Identities#

1.1.4.1. Trigonmetric functions#

(1.11)#\[\begin{align} \sin \theta &= \frac{y}{r} & \csc \theta &= \frac{r}{x} = \frac{1}{\cos \theta} \\ \cos \theta &= \frac{x}{r} & \sec \theta &= \frac{r}{x} = \frac{1}{\cos \theta} \\ \tan \theta &= \frac{y}{x} = \frac{\sin \theta}{\cos \theta} & \cot \theta &= \frac{x}{y} = \frac{\cos \theta}{\sin \theta} \end{align}\]
(1.12)#\[\begin{align} \sin^2 \theta + \cos^2 \theta = 1 \\ 1 + \tan^2 \theta = \sec^2 \theta \\ 1 + \cot^2 \theta = \csc^2 \theta \end{align}\]
(1.13)#\[\begin{align} \cos(A+B) = \cos A \cos B - \sin A \sin B \\ \sin(A+B) = \sin A \cos B - \cos A \sin B \end{align}\]
(1.14)#\[\begin{align} \cos 2 \theta = \cos^2 \theta - \sin^2 \theta \\ \sin 2 \theta = 2 \sin \theta \cos \theta \end{align}\]
(1.15)#\[\begin{align} \cos^2 \theta = \frac{1 + \cos 2 \theta}{2} \\ \sin^2 \theta = \frac{1 - \cos 2 \theta}{2} \end{align}\]
(1.16)#\[\begin{align} \sin(\theta + 2 \pi) = \sin \theta \\ \cos(\theta + 2 \pi) = \cos \theta \end{align}\]
(1.17)#\[\begin{align} \sin(- \theta) = - \sin \theta \\ \cos(- \theta) = cos \theta \end{align}\]

1.1.4.2. Exponential functions#

(1.18)#\[\begin{align} a^x a^y &= a^{x+y} \\ \frac{a^x}{a^y} &= a^{x-y} \\ (a^x)^y = (a^y)^x &= a^{xy} \\ a^x b^x &= (ab)^x \\ \frac{a^x}{b^x} &= \left (\frac{a}{b} \right)^x \end{align}\]

1.1.4.3. Logarithmic functions#

Definition:

(1.19)#\[\begin{align} y &= \log_{a}x \\ x &= a^y \end{align}\]

Natural Log:

(1.20)#\[\begin{equation} \ln x = \log_{e} x \end{equation}\]

Common log:

(1.21)#\[\begin{equation} \log x = \log_{10} x \end{equation}\]
(1.22)#\[\begin{align} \ln(bx) &= \ln(b) + \ln(x) \\ \ln\left(\frac{b}{x}\right) &= \ln(b) - \ln(x) \\ \ln(x^r) &= r \ln(x) \\ \log_{a}x &= \frac{\ln(x)}{\ln(a)} \\ \end{align}\]
