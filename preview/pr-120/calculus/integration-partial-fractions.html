1.9. Partial fraction decomposition#

Some ODEs give rise to integrals like:

(1.35)#\[\begin{equation} y' = \frac{3x+11}{x^2-x-6} \to y = \int \frac{3x+11}{x^2-x-6} \, dx \end{equation}\]

This cannot be done with u-substitution… how to take it?

(1.36)#\[\begin{equation} \frac{3x+11}{x^2-x-6} =\frac{3x+11}{(x-3)(x+2)} = \frac{A_1}{x-3} + \frac{A_2}{x+2} \end{equation}\]
(1.37)#\[\begin{align} 3x+11 &= A_1(x+2) + A_2(x-3) \\ &= (A_1 + A_2)x + (2A_1 - 3A_2) \\ \end{align}\]
(1.38)#\[\begin{align} A_1 + A_2 = 3 \end{align}\]
(1.39)#\[\begin{align} 2A_1 - 3A_2 = 11 \end{align}\]
(1.40)#\[\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 3\\ 2 & -3 & 11 \end{bmatrix} \to \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 3\\ 0 & -5 & 5 \end{bmatrix} \to \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 3\\ 0 & 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \to \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 4\\ 0 & 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix}\]
(1.41)#\[\begin{align} A_1=4 A_2=-1 \end{align}\]
(1.42)#\[\begin{align} y&=\int\left( \frac{4}{x-3}-\frac{1}{x+2} \right)dx \\ &= 4\ln| x-3 | - \ln|x+2| + C \end{align}\]

1.9.1. Partial Fraction Decomposition#

(1.43)#\[\begin{align} Procedure: For a rational polynomial \frac{P\left(x\right)}{Q\left(x\right)} where Q is expressed using either linear \left(ax+b \right) or irreducible quadratic \left(ax^2 + bx + c\right) factors and the degree of P is less than Q \end{align}\]
(1.44)#\[\begin{align} ax+b \to \frac{A}{ax+b} \end{align}\]
(1.45)#\[\begin{align} \left(ax+b \right)^k \to \frac{A_1}{ax+b} + \frac{A_2}{\left(ax+b \right)^2}+ ...+ \frac{A_k}{\left(ax+b \right)^k} \end{align}\]
(1.46)#\[\begin{align} ax^2 + bx + c \to \frac{Ax+B}{ax^2+bx+c} \end{align}\]
(1.47)#\[\begin{align} \left(ax^2 + bx+c \right)^k: repeat like for linear factor \end{align} \]
(1.48)#\[\begin{equation} y'= \frac{x^2-29x+5}{\left(x-4 \right)^2 \left(x^2+3 \right)} \end{equation}\]
(1.49)#\[\begin{equation} \frac{x^2-29x+5}{\left(x-4 \right)^2 \left(x^2+3 \right)} = \frac{A_1}{x-4} + \frac{A_2}{(x-4)^2} + \frac{A_3x+ B_3}{x^2+3} \end{equation}\]
(1.50)#\[\begin{equation} x^2-29x+5 = A_1 \left(x-4 \right) \left(x^2+3 \right) + A_2 \left(x^2+3 \right)+ \left(A_3x+B_3 \right) \left(x-4 \right)^2 \end{equation}\]
(1.51)#\[\begin{equation} =A_1 \left(x^3-4x^2+3x-12 \right)+A_2 \left(x^2+3 \right)+\left(A_3x+B_3 \right)\left(x^2-8x+16 \right) \end{equation}\]
(1.52)#\[\begin{equation} =\left(A_1+A_3\right)x^3+\left(-4A_1+A_2-8A_3+B_3\right)x^2 + \left(3A_1+16A_3-8B_3\right)x+\left(-12A_1+3A_2+16B_3\right) \end{equation}\]
(1.53)#\[\begin{equation} A_1+A_3 = 0 \end{equation}\]
(1.54)#\[\begin{equation} -4A_1 + A_2-8A_3 + B _3 =1 \end{equation}\]
(1.55)#\[\begin{equation} 3A_1 + 16A_3 - 8B_3 = -29 \end{equation}\]
(1.56)#\[\begin{equation} $-12A_1+3A_2+16B_3 =5 \end{equation}\]

\(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 1 & 0\\ -4 & 1 & -8 & 1\\ 3 & 0 & 16 & -8\\ -12 & 3 & 0 & 16 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} A_1 \\ A_2\\ A_3\\ B_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0\\ 1\\ -29 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix}\) \(\to\) \(A_1 = 1, A_2 = -5, A_3 = -1, B_3 =2\)

(1.57)#\[\begin{equation} y= \int\left(\frac{1}{x-4}-\frac{5}{\left(x-4\right)^2}-\frac{x}{x^2+3}+\frac{2}{x^2+3}\right) dx \end{equation}\]
(1.58)#\[\begin{equation} \arctan(x)= \int \left(\frac{1}{1+x^2}\right)dx \end{equation}\]
(1.59)#\[\begin{equation} = \ln|x-4|+\frac{5}{x-4} -\frac{1}{2}ln (x^2 + 3)+\frac{2}{\sqrt{3}} \arctan(\frac{x}{\sqrt{3}}) + C \end{equation}\]
