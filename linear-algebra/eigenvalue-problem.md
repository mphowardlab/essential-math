# Eigenvalue problem
2. Multiple Eigenvalues
   
An nxn matrix has n eigenvalues, but they may not be distinct!

$\underline{\underline{A}}$ = 

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
-2 & 2 & 3 \\
2 & 1 & -6 \\
-1 & -2 & 0
\end{bmatrix}
$$

| $\underline{\underline{A}}$ - $\lambda$  $\underline{\underline{I}}$| =

$$
\left|\begin{matrix}
-2-\lambda & 2 & 3 \\
2 & 1-\lambda & -6 \\
-1 & -2 & -\lambda
\end{matrix} \right|
$$ 

=
 
$$
-1
\left|\begin{matrix}
2 & 3 \\
1-\lambda & -6
\end{matrix} \right|
$$ 

$$
-(-2)
\left|\begin{matrix}
-2-\lambda & 2 & 3 \\
2 & 1-\lambda & -6 \\
-1 & -2 & -\lambda
\end{matrix} \right|
$$
