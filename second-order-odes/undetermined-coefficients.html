7.5. Undetermined coefficients#

Undetermined coefficients

If a nonhomogeneous linear second-order ODE has constant coefficients

(7.84)#\[\begin{equation} y'' + a y' + b y = r(x) \end{equation}\]

we can “guess” a solution as follows:

\(r\)

\(y_{\rm p}\)

\(k e^{\gamma x}\)

\(k e^{\gamma x}\)

\(k x^n (n=0,1,\ldots)\)

\(k_n x^n + k_{n-1} x^{n-1} + \cdots + k_1 x + k_0\)

\(k e^{\alpha x} \cos{\omega x}\)

\(e^{\alpha x}(k_1 \cos{\omega x} + k_2 \sin{\omega x})\)

\(k e^{\alpha x} \sin{\omega x}\)

\(e^{\alpha x}(k_1 \cos{\omega x} + k_2 \sin{\omega x})\)

  • If r has multiple terms, add guesses for each together.

  • If the guess for \(y_{\rm p}\) is a homogeneous solution \(y_{\rm h}\), multiply by x unless this solution is a repeated root - then multiply by \(x^2\)!

For example, to solve:

(7.85)#\[\begin{equation} y'' + y = 0.001 x^2, \quad y(0) = 0, \quad y'(0) = 1.5 \end{equation}\]

Use the following steps:

  1. Find \(y_{\rm h}\):

    (7.86)#\[\begin{align} y_{\rm h}'' + y_{\rm h} &= 0 \\ \lambda^2 + 1 &= 0 \end{align}\]

    so \(\lambda = \pm i\) and

    (7.87)#\[\begin{equation} y_{\rm h} = c_1 \cos x +c_2 \sin x \end{equation}\]

  2. Guess \(y_{\rm p}\) and solve for undetermined coefficients:

    (7.88)#\[\begin{align} y_{p} &= k_2 x^2 + k_1 x + k_0 \\ y_{\rm p}' &= 2k_2 x + k_1 \\ y_{\rm p}'' &= 2k_2 \end{align}\]

    So

    (7.89)#\[\begin{equation} y_{\rm p}'' + y_{p} = 2 k_2 + k_2 x^2 + k_1 x + k_0 = 0.001x^2 \end{equation}\]

    Equating coefficients of like powers of x gives the system

    (7.90)#\[\begin{align} k_2 &= 0.001 \\ k_1 &= 0 \\ 2k_2 + k_0 &= 0 \\ \end{align}\]

    which gives \(k_0 = -0.002\).

  3. Combine solutions and solve IVP.

    The general solution and its first derivative are:

    (7.91)#\[\begin{align} y &= y_{\rm h} + y_{\rm p} = c_1 \cos x + c_2 \sin x + 0.001x^2 - 0.002 \\ y' &= -c_1 \sin x + c_2 \cos x + 0.002x \end{align}\]

    Substituting in the first boundary condition:

    (7.92)#\[\begin{align} y(0) &= c_1 - 0.002 = 0\\ y'(0) &= c_2 = 1.5 \end{align}\]

    Hence, \(c_1 = 0.002\), \(c_2 = 1.5\), and

    (7.93)#\[\begin{equation} y = 0.002 \cos x + 1.5 \sin x + 0.001x^2 - 0.002 \end{equation}\]

As a more complex example, let’s find the form of the particular solution for:

(7.94)#\[\begin{equation} y'' + 3y' + 2.25y = -10e^-1.5x + \cos x \end{equation}\]

  1. Find \(y_{\rm h}\):

    (7.95)#\[\begin{align} y_{\rm h}''+3y_{\rm h}'+2.25y_{\rm h} &= 0 \\ \lambda^2 +3\lambda+2.25 = 0 \\ (\lambda+1.5)^2 &= 0 \\ \end{align}\]

    so \(\lambda = -1.5\). This is a real repeated root, so

    (7.96)#\[\begin{equation} y_{\rm h} = (c_1 + c_2 x)e^{-1.5x} \end{equation}\]

  2. Guess \(y_{\rm p}\):

    We should add guesses for both terms in r. Our first term is an exponential, but it needs to be multiplied by \(x^2\) because it is the same as the homogeneous solution, which is a real repeated root. Our second term is a sum of cosine and sine.

    (7.97)#\[\begin{align} y_{\rm p} = k_1 x^2 e^{-1.5 x} + (k_2 \cos x +k_3 \sin x) \end{align}\]

7.5.1. Skill builder problems#

  1. Solve:

    (7.98)#\[\begin{equation} y'' + 3y' + 2y = 30e^{2x}, \quad y(0) = 1, \quad y'(0) = 0 \end{equation}\]
    Solution

    1. Find homogeneous solution \(y_{\rm h}\):

      (7.99)#\[\begin{align} y_{\rm h}'' + 3y_{\rm h}' + 2y_{\rm h} &= 0 \\ \lambda^{2} + 3\lambda + 2 &= 0 \\ (\lambda + 1)(\lambda + 2) &= 0 \end{align}\]

      so \(\lambda_1 = -1\) and \(\lambda_2 = -2\). Then,

      (7.100)#\[\begin{equation} y_{\rm h} = c_1 e^{x} + c_2 e^{-2x} \end{equation}\]

    2. Find particular solution \(y_{\rm p}\):

      (7.101)#\[\begin{align} y_{\rm p} &= ke^{2x} \\ y_{\rm p}' &= 2ke^{2x} \\ y_{\rm p}'' &= 4ke^{2x} \end{align}\]

      Combine and solve for k:

      (7.102)#\[\begin{align} 4ke^{2x} e^{2x} &+ 6ke^{2x} e^{2x} + 2ke^{2x} e^{2x} = 30e^2x e^{2x} \\ 12k &= 30 \end{align}\]

      so \(k = 5/2\) and

      (7.103)#\[\begin{equation} y_{\rm p} = \frac{5}{2} e^{2x} \end{equation}\]

    3. Combine and apply initial conditions:

      The general solution and its first derivative are

      (7.104)#\[\begin{align} y &= c_1 e^{-x} + c_2 e^-{2x} + \frac{5}{2} e^{2x} \\ y' &= -c_1 e^{-x} - 2c_2 e^{-2x} + 5e^{2x} \end{align}\]

      Plugging in initial conditions

      (7.105)#\[\begin{align} y(0) &= c_1 + c_2 + \frac{5}{2} = 1 \\ y'(0) &= -c_1 - 2c_2 + 5 = 0 \end{align}\]

      This is a system of linear equations:

      (7.106)#\[\begin{align} c_1 + c_2 &= -\frac{3}{2} \\ c_1 + 2c_2 &= 5 \\ \end{align}\]

      that can be solved to give \(c_1 = -8\) and \(c_2 = 13/2\).

    The final solution is:

    (7.107)#\[\begin{equation} y = -8e^{-x} + \frac{13}{2} e^{-2x} = \frac{5}{2} e^{2x} \end{equation}\]

  2. Solve:

    (7.108)#\[\begin{equation} y'' + 4y = 16 \cos 2x , \quad y(0), \quad y'(0) = 0 \end{equation}\]
    Solution

    1. Find homogeneous solution \(y_{\rm h}\):

      (7.109)#\[\begin{align} y_{\rm h}'' + 4y_{\rm h} &= 0 \\ \lambda^{2} + 4 &= 0 \\ \end{align}\]

      so \(\lambda_{1,2} = \pm 2i\) and

      (7.110)#\[\begin{equation} y_{\rm h} = c_1 \cos 2x + c_2 \sin 2x \end{equation}\]

    2. Find the particular solution \(y_{\rm p}\):

      (7.111)#\[\begin{align} y_{\rm p} &= x(k_1 \cos 2x + k_2 \sin 2x) \\ y_{\rm p}' &= x(-2k_1 \sin 2x + 2k_2 \cos 2x) + (k_1 \cos 2x + k_2 \sin 2x) \\ y_{\rm p}'' &= x(-4k_1 \cos 2x - 4k_2 \sin 2x) + 2(-2k_1 \sin 2x + 2k_2 \cos 2x) \end{align}\]

      Combine:

      (7.112)#\[\begin{equation} y_{\rm p}'' + 4y_{\rm p} = 2(-2k_1 \sin 2x + 2k_2 \cos 2x) = 16\cos 2x \end{equation}\]

      Comparing terms gives \(k_1 = 0\) and \(k_2 = 4\).

    3. Combine and apply initial conditions:

      The general solution and its first derivative are

      (7.113)#\[\begin{align} y &= c_1 \cos 2x + c_2 \sin 2x + 4x \sin 2x \\ y' &= -2c_1 \sin 2x + 2c_2 \cos 2x + 4x(2 \cos 2x) + 4 \sin 2x \end{align}\]

      Plugging in the initial conditions:

      (7.114)#\[\begin{align} y(0) &= c_1 = 0 \\ y'(0) &= 2c_2 = 0 \end{align}\]

      gives \(c_1 = 0\) and \(c_2 = 0\).

    The final solution is:

    (7.115)#\[\begin{equation} y = 4x \sin 2x \end{equation}\]

  3. Solve:

    (7.116)#\[\begin{equation} y'' - y' - 12y = 144x^3 + \frac{25}{2}, \quad y(0) = 5, \quad y'(0) = -1/2 \end{equation}\]
    Solution

    1. Find homogeneous solution \(y_{\rm h}\):

      (7.117)#\[\begin{align} y_{\rm h}'' - y_{\rm h}' - 12y_{\rm h} &= 0 \lambda^2 - \lambda - 12 &= 0 (\lambda - 4)(\lambda + 3) &= 0 \end{align}\]

      so \(\lambda_1 = 4\), \(\lambda_2 = -3\), and

      (7.118)#\[\begin{equation} y_{\rm h} = c_1 e^{4x} + c_2 e^{-3x} \end{equation}\]

    2. Find the particular solution \(y_{\rm p}\):

      (7.119)#\[\begin{align} y_{\rm p} &= k_1 x^3 + k_2 x^2 + k_3 x + k_4 \\ y_{\rm p}' &= 3k_1x^2 + 2k_2x + k_3 \\ y_{\rm p}'' &= 6k_1x + 2k_2 \end{align}\]

      Substitute in ODE and combine:

      (7.120)#\[\begin{align} & y_{\rm p}'' - y_{\rm p}' - 12y_{\rm p} \\ &= (6k_1x + 2k_2) - (3k_1x^2 + 2k_2x + k_3) - 12(k_1x^3 + k_2x^2 + k_3x + k_4) \\ &= -12k_1x^3 + (-3k_1 - 12k_2)x^2 + (6k_1 - 2k_2 - 12k_3)x + (2k_2 - k_3 - 12k_4) \end{align}\]

      Comparing coefficients of powers of x gives:

      (7.121)#\[\begin{align} -12k_1 &= 144 \\ -3k_1 - 12k_2 &= 0 \\ 36 - 12k_2 &= 0 \\ 6k_1 - 2k_2 - 12k_3 &= 0 \\ 2k_2 - k_3 - 12k_4 &= \frac{25}{2} \end{align}\]

      Solving this system of linear equations gives \(k_1 = -12\), \(k_2 = 3\), \(k_3 = -13/2\), and \(k_4 = 0\), so:

      (7.122)#\[\begin{equation} y_{\rm p} = -12x_3 + 3x^2 - \frac{13}{2}x \end{equation}\]

    3. Combine and apply boundary conditions:

      The general solution and its first derivative are

      (7.123)#\[\begin{align} y &= c_1 e^{4x} + c_2 e^{-3x} - 12x^3 + 3x^2 - \frac{13}{2}x \\ y' &= 4c_1 e^{4x} - 3c_2e^{-3X} - 36x^2 + 6x - \frac{13}{2} \end{align}\]

      Plugging in the initial conditions:

      (7.124)#\[\begin{align} y(0) &= c_1 + c_2 = 5 \\ y'(0) &= 4c_1 - 3c_2 - \frac{13}{2} = -\frac{1}{2} \end{align}\]

      Solve using matrices:

      (7.125)#\[\begin{align} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 5 \\ 4 & -3 & 6 \end{bmatrix} \begin{matrix} \vphantom{R_1} \\ -4 R_1 \end{matrix} &\to \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 5 \\ 0 & -7 & 14 \end{bmatrix} \begin{matrix} \vphantom{R_1} \\ \div -7 \end{matrix} \\ &\to \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 5 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{matrix} -R_2 \\ \vphantom{R_1} \end{matrix} \\ &\to \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 3 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \end{align}\]

      so \(c_1 = 3\) and \(c_2 = 2\). The final solution is:

      (7.126)#\[\begin{equation} y = 3e^{4x} + 2e^{-3x} - 12x^3 + 3x^2 - \frac{13}{2}x \end{equation}\]
